What is Bullying?

Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. It can take many forms, including physical, verbal, social, and cyberbullying.

How to Respond to Bullying

  • Stay Calm: Try not to react with anger.
  • Document Incidents: Keep a record of bullying incidents.
  • Talk to Someone: Share your experience with a trusted friend or adult.
  • Report the Behavior: Use our platform to report incidents.

Common Misconceptions about Bullying

Many behaviors may not seem like bullying but can still be harmful:

  • Exclusion: Ignoring someone or leaving them out of activities.
  • Gossiping: Spreading rumors about someone.
  • Teasing: Making jokes at someone else's expense.

How to Deal with Bullying

  • Stand Up for Yourself: Assertively tell the bully to stop.
  • Seek Support: Join support groups or talk to a counselor.
  • Educate Yourself: Learn about the effects of bullying.
